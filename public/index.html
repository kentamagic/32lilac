JUSTINE YAN,
Curator
A few afternoon conversations at Book Trader Cafe, a few loose ambitions to do something before we graduate—that’s how this show was conceived.

And from there, it was a rapid accumulation of ideas and questions:
If I give an artist a bedroom, how will she fill that room? If I give an artist a bathroom, how will she fill that room?

We just thought it’d be really cool.
BECKY ASTON,
Master Bedroom
I explore the confusion of space, norms and time that comes from inhabiting multiple places.The act of building a home, the act of calling another generic, empty space a home for the few moments you occupy it is what I am both embracing and fighting against.

OLIVIA SCHWOB,
2F Bathrooms
Using my own form as an index and an image-making device, I ask: how can we know what goes on inside us? How is the act of imaging the body, inside and out, inherently violent? How can we come to accept the unreliability of our perceptions and images of our bodies?
JACQI LI,
2F Skylight Sitting Area
My work uses cloth to explore the creation of space and form from the fragile and intangible, tension between weight and weightlessness, and a spiritual desire for unity and support. Through a kind of reverse weaving, I pull apart the grid of a fabric in order to draw out an organic, weblike pattern.
